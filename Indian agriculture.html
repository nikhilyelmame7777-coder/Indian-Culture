Production and Ranking

India is a global agricultural powerhouse, ranking second worldwide in farm output contributing roughly US$481 billion (2020) in value and holding the largest net cultivated area. In 2024-25, it achieved record foodgrain production of 357.73 MMT, with top global rankings as the leading producer of milk, pulses, and spices, and 2nd in rice, wheat, sugarcane, and vegetables.

Government Schemes

Indian government initiatives in agriculture focus on enhancing income, reducing input costs, and ensuring price security for farmers. Key initiatives include PM-KISAN (direct income support), PM Fasal Bima Yojana (crop insurance), e-NAM (digital markets), Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (post-harvest loans), and PM Krishi Sinchai Yojana (irrigation). These measures promote sustainable farming and improve market access.

Transforming Indian Agriculture

Contribution to GDP and employment details :The Indian economy, projected to grow at 7.4% in FY26, is dominated by the service sector (53–56% of GDP), while agriculture remains the largest employer, engaging over 40% of the workforce. The industrial sector contributes roughly 30% of GDP, with manufacturing, agriculture, and service sectors driving diverse growth across rural and urban economies. India recorded the lowest inflation rate since the beginning of the CPI series, with April-December 2025 average headline inflation coming in at 1.7%, attributing to general disinflationary trend in food and fuel prices. Among major Emerging Markets & Developing Economies (EMDEs), India has recorded one of the sharpest declines in headline inflation in 2025 over 2024, amounting to about 1.8 percentage points.

Croping Seasons in India

Kharif Crobs
  • Sowing:July - October
  • Harvest - September to October
  • A.k.a Monsoon Crops
  • Such crops require a lot of water
  • Example: rice, sorghum, maize, tea, rubber, coffee, guar, Sesame, cereals such as Arhar Dhal, pearl millet, soybeans, cotton, oilseeds, etc.

Rabi Crops
  • Sowing: October - November
  • Harvest: March - April
  • A.k.a Winter Crops
  • Such crops require less water and cooler climate
  • Example: wheat, barley, mustard, peas, gram, linseed, etc.

Zaid Crops
  • Sowing: March - April
  • Harvest: June - July
  • A.k.a Summer Crops
  • Grown in short season between Rabi and Kharif
  • Such crops require warm and dry climate
  • Example: watermelon, muskmelon, cucumber, vegetables, etc.

Crop Varieties

India boasts a diverse range of crop varieties, categorized by season (Kharif, Rabi, Zaid) and purpose (food, cash, horticultural). Key varieties released by ICAR include climate-resilient options (e.g., drought-tolerant rice/wheat), nutrition-rich pulses, and high-yielding oilseeds, with over 286 special varieties released since 2014 for abiotic stress tolerance.
