🏑 Hockey

1. Types of Hockey

  • Field Hockey: Played with 11 players per side on grass or turf.
  • Ice Hockey: Played on ice with usually 6 players per side.

2. Equipment

  • Hockey stick
  • Protective guards (shin guards, helmets)
  • Gloves
  • Ball (field hockey) or puck (ice hockey)

3. Gameplay

A standard field hockey match lasts 60 minutes, divided into four 15-minute quarters.

4. Governing Bodies

  • International Hockey Federation (FIH): Manages global field hockey.
  • Hockey India: Oversees hockey activities in India.

5. Recent Developments (2026)

  • India: Punjab and Odisha secured medals at national championships.
  • Women’s Team: Played competitive series in Buenos Aires.
  • International: Argentina won gold in both men’s and women’s at the Junior Pan American Cup.

6. Key Terminology

  • Puck: A hard rubber disk used in ice hockey.
  • Short Corner (Penalty Corner): A common penalty in field hockey.
  • Blue Line: Divides attacking and defensive zones in ice hockey.

🏏 Cricket

1. Types of Cricket

  • Test Cricket: Played over 5 days with unlimited overs.
  • One Day Internationals (ODI): 50 overs per side.
  • T20 Cricket: Fast-paced format with 20 overs per side.

2. Equipment

  • Cricket bat
  • Ball
  • Stumps and bails
  • Protective gear (pads, gloves, helmet)

3. Gameplay

Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players. One team bats while the other bowls and fields. The objective is to score more runs than the opponent.

4. Governing Bodies

  • International Cricket Council (ICC): Governs international cricket.
  • BCCI (India): Manages cricket in India.

5. Recent Developments (2026)

  • India: Strong performances in international series across formats.
  • IPL 2026: Continues to be one of the most popular T20 leagues worldwide.
  • Global: Increased focus on T20 leagues and young talent development.

6. Key Terminology

  • Over: A set of 6 balls bowled by a bowler.
  • Wicket: Can mean dismissal of a batsman or the stumps.
  • Run: Basic unit of scoring in cricket.
  • Boundary: Ball reaching the edge of the field (4 or 6 runs).

🏃 Kho Kho

1. Type

Traditional Indian tag sport played between two teams.

2. Equipment

  • No major equipment required
  • Marked rectangular field

3. Gameplay

Teams alternate between chasing and defending. Players aim to tag opponents quickly.

4. Governing Body

Kho Kho Federation of India

5. Terminology

  • Pole: Used to change direction
  • Kho: Signal to pass chasing role

🤼 Kabaddi

1. Type

Contact team sport popular in India.

2. Equipment

  • Mat or ground court

3. Gameplay

A raider enters opponent’s half, tags players, and returns without being caught.

4. Governing Body

International Kabaddi Federation

5. Terminology

  • Raid: Attacking move
  • Tackle: Defending move
